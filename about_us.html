The "Reactive" Trap

It started with the phone ringing. Working inside a municipal Public Works department, our founder watched the same scene play out daily. A resident calls: "There’s a raised sidewalk on 3rd Street."

For the city staff, this triggered a scramble. Is it new? Did we already inspect it? Who is free to go check? Despite working tirelessly, the department was trapped in a reactive loop—always chasing yesterday's complaints, never getting ahead of tomorrow's risks.

The Shift to Proactive

We realized the chaos wasn't a failure of effort; it was a failure of visibility. You can't manage what you haven't measured.

"Imagine a resident calls about a trip hazard, and instead of saying 'We'll send someone out,' you say: 'We have that logged. It’s a Priority 2 hazard, and crews are scheduled for August.' That shift changes everything."
Founder & Lead Data Specialist

Questin Francis

Questin Francis is not a typical high-level consultant who sits in a boardroom. His expertise is rooted in the daily operations of municipal work. Working directly alongside inspectors and city engineers, he gained hands-on experience in everything from industrial backflow inspections to assisting with budget studies and water rights administration.

This diverse background includes operational experience supporting the data collection for EPA-mandated lead pipe surveys. In this role, Questin acted as the vital link between "boots on the ground" field crews and the central GIS dashboards.

Today, Questin combines this municipal experience with advanced data engineering capabilities, ensuring your data is clean, accurate, and compatible.
